Summary

At approximately 02:13, the west corridor plaster behind Panel W-17 began to move. Not crack. Not bow. Move, as if the wall had learned to breathe. The sound was first reported as a "wet whisper," then as "someone practicing our name."

The corridor was cleared. The corridor did not stay empty. Two staff members claimed they heard footsteps that matched their own pace exactly one half-step behind, like a shadow trying to remember how to walk.

Note: The wall did not open. It only made room. A mount-shape formed where no seam exists on any blueprint we possess.

Incident Narrative

Security footage shows W-17 at 02:12:58: normal. At 02:13:01: the camera's timestamp skips forward by nineteen seconds while the corridor lighting remains stable. The system claims no dropouts.

At 02:13:20, a maintenance cart rolls into view from off-camera. There is no operator. It stops directly in front of W-17. The cart's top drawer opens on its own. Tools inside shift as though something has gently stirred them with a finger.

At 02:13:44, Audio Team reports a voice on the corridor microphone: "We're being quiet so you don't have to be." Audio Team insists the phrase was not broadcast from any speaker in the building.

RECOVERED TRANSCRIPTION (PARTIAL) - MIC C3 [unidentified] : you can stop pretending now [unidentified] : the building remembers you better than you do [unidentified] : please do not bring lights [unidentified] : it hurts when you make it see

Rules (Effective Immediately)

  • Do not address W-17 directly. Do not respond if it uses your name.
  • Do not approach with flashlights, headlamps, or phone screens uncovered.
  • Do not bring mirrors. Do not bring reflective tools.
  • If you hear your footsteps doubled, stop and count to ten without turning around.
  • If you smell bleach or pennies, leave the corridor. Do not run.

- R. H. Facilities Liaison (If you receive a second copy of this memo that you did not request, destroy it without reading. It is not meant for you. It is practicing.)

Unusual Observations

  • Paint near W-17 dries in spirals, even when applied flat.
  • Dust patterns form concentric rings overnight, like something nested there.
  • Corridor temperature remains constant, but staff report "cold in the bones."
  • Meta fasteners corrode only on the side facing the panel.
FIELD NOTE - SHIFT A The mouth isn't always there. But the space for it is. Sometimes you can feel the room waiting.
