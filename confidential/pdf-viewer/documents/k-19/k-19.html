Summary

K-19 presents as a vertical "spine" of matte material, roughly human-height, emerging from standing water. Observers describe it as a ship's keel turned upright, but warmer than it should be.

K-19 does not chase. It "corrects." When a person enters its vicinity, their posture subtly changes: shoulders square, chin lifts, breathing slows. Those affected report calm certainty and a sudden desire to "walk straight."

Primary hazard: K-19 gradually removes "unnecessary" movement from a subject. This includes fidgeting, blinking, and (in later stages) swallowing.

Incident Narrative

On 2006-09-03, Room ███ flooded due to a valve failure. When Facilities arrived, the waterline was mirror-smooth despite visible inflow. Three tools placed on the surface did not sink.

A security camera recorded a staff member stepping in, pausing, and standing perfectly still for 11 minutes. When asked why, the staff member answered: "It fixed my balance. I didn't want to ruin it."

RECOVERED VOICE NOTE (PARTIAL) I can feel it aligning me. Like a level tool inside my bones. I think it's proud when I stay still. It's... helping. It's helping too much.

Handling Rules

  • Do not enter standing water within K-19's wing.
  • Do not "test" stillness. Do not hold your breath near it.
  • If you feel calm "for no reason," exit immediately and speak aloud one unnecessary sentence.
  • Chew gum during inspections. It disrupts the effect.
  • If you notice fewer blinks, contact Medical before you finish the report.

- Intake Desk, Night If you see K-19 outside the water, it is not K-19. It is you being measured.

Unusual Observations

  • Water near K-19 becomes acoustically "quiet."
  • Subjects report hearing a faint ship-horn in their teeth.
  • Camera footage near the entity loses micro-jitter.
  • Nearby signage shifts to perfect alignment overnight.
